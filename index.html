Key Adjustments & Tips
1
⚡
Progressive Overload is #1
Track your lifts weekly. Add weight or a rep each session. In a deficit, maintaining strength = maintaining muscle. Don't chase soreness — chase progression.
2
🚴
Bike Cardio Strategy
20 min post-lift at moderate resistance (Zone 2, ~130–145 BPM) burns ~180–260 cal. Keep it conversational. On leg days, drop resistance slightly to avoid compounding fatigue.
3
😴
Wednesday Rest Matters
With Push Mon, Pull Tue, the Wednesday rest gives your upper body 48 hrs before Legs Thursday. This is a smart natural split — don't feel the urge to add anything on Wednesday.
4
⛳
Golf as Active Recovery
Driving range (120 balls) = ~250–350 cal. A cart round = ~300–400 cal. Wednesday or Sunday work best. Hip hinge and rotational core work in the gym directly transfers to your swing.
5
📊
Weigh Weekly, Not Daily
Daily weight swings 1–3 lbs from water, sodium, and carbs. Take a weekly Monday morning fasted weight. After 4 weeks you should see a clear ~1 lb/week downward trend.
6
🔁
Deload Every 6–8 Weeks
Drop to 3–4 days, 60% of normal volume. At 6 days/week in a caloric deficit, accumulated fatigue is real. A deload week prevents injury and often leads to strength PRs the following week.
